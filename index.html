Math & Core Tools
Math Foundations

These are the core relations you will keep using across CFD, FEA, and vibrations.

Algebra and calculus essentials

Derivatives
\( \frac{df}{dx} \approx \frac{f(x+\Delta x)-f(x)}{\Delta x} \)

Rate of change. In engineering, this shows how a variable changes in space or time.

Integrals
\( \int_a^b f(x)\,dx \)

Accumulated quantity. Common in work, energy, mass, and momentum calculations.

Ordinary differential equations
\( a_n \frac{d^n y}{dt^n} + a_{n-1}\frac{d^{n-1}y}{dt^{n-1}} + \dots + a_1\frac{dy}{dt} + a_0 y = f(t) \)

Used heavily in dynamics, vibrations, heat transfer, and control.

Linear algebra
\( [K]\{x\}=\{F\} \)

This one shows up everywhere in FEA and modal analysis.

Python & MATLAB for Mechanical Analysis

This page connects the theory to actual computation.

When to use MATLAB vs Python

MATLAB
Fast matrix work, plotting, built-in engineering toolboxes, very good for academic analysis
Python
Flexible, open-source, strong ecosystem for scientific computing, automation, optimization, and data pipelines
Python example: solving a linear system
import numpy as np

K = np.array([[10, -2], [-2, 5]])
F = np.array([100, 0])
x = np.linalg.solve(K, F)
print(x)
MATLAB example: same idea
K = [10 -2; -2 5];
F = [100; 0];
x = K \ F
disp(x)
CFD
CFD Basics

Computational fluid dynamics solves fluid flow equations numerically over a mesh.

Core concepts

  • Control volume
  • Mesh / grid
  • Boundary conditions
  • Solver
  • Convergence
Fluid property formulas
\( \rho = \frac{m}{V} \)
\( \mu = \text{dynamic viscosity} \)
\( \nu = \frac{\mu}{\rho} \)
These properties control inertia, diffusion, and resistance to deformation in flow.
CFD
Governing Equations

These are the equations CFD is actually solving under the hood.

Continuity equation

\( \frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot (\rho \vec{V}) = 0 \)

Conservation of mass. For incompressible flow, this reduces to:

\( \nabla \cdot \vec{V} = 0 \)
Momentum equation
\( \rho \left( \frac{\partial \vec{V}}{\partial t} + \vec{V}\cdot\nabla\vec{V} \right) = -\nabla p + \mu \nabla^2 \vec{V} + \rho \vec{g} \)

That is the Navier-Stokes equation for a Newtonian fluid in compact form.

Energy equation
\( \rho c_p \left( \frac{\partial T}{\partial t} + \vec{V}\cdot\nabla T \right) = k\nabla^2T + \dot{q} \)
CFD
Dimensionless Numbers

These numbers tell you what physics dominates the flow.

Essential dimensionless groups

Re = \( \frac{\rho V L}{\mu} = \frac{VL}{\nu} \)

Reynolds number: inertia vs viscosity.

Pr = \( \frac{\nu}{\alpha} \)

Prandtl number: momentum diffusivity vs thermal diffusivity.

Ma = \( \frac{V}{a} \)

Mach number: flow speed relative to speed of sound.

Nu = \( \frac{hL}{k} \)

Nusselt number: convective vs conductive heat transfer.

CFD
Numerical Methods in CFD

These are the methods that turn PDEs into solvable algebraic equations.

Discretization basics

Finite difference derivative
\( \frac{du}{dx}\bigg|_i \approx \frac{u_{i+1}-u_i}{\Delta x} \)
Central difference
\( \frac{du}{dx}\bigg|_i \approx \frac{u_{i+1}-u_{i-1}}{2\Delta x} \)
Transient forward time step
\( \frac{u^{n+1}-u^n}{\Delta t} \)
You need to understand numerical stability, truncation error, and convergence here, or the solver will happily produce nonsense with confidence.

Solid Mechanics Foundations

Before FEA, the continuum mechanics meaning has to be clear.

Basic mechanics relations

\( \sigma = \frac{F}{A} \)

Normal stress.

\( \tau = \frac{V}{A} \)

Average shear stress.

\( \epsilon = \frac{\Delta L}{L} \)

Normal strain.

\( \gamma \approx \tan\phi \)

Engineering shear strain.

FEA / Mechanical Analysis
Stress, Strain, and Constitutive Relations

These relations connect loading to deformation.

Hooke's law

\( \sigma = E\epsilon \)

Uniaxial linear elasticity.

Shear relation
\( \tau = G\gamma \)
Elastic constants
\( E = 2G(1+\nu) \)
\( K = \frac{E}{3(1-2\nu)} \)
Von Mises stress
\( \sigma_v = \sqrt{\frac{(\sigma_1-\sigma_2)^2 + (\sigma_2-\sigma_3)^2 + (\sigma_3-\sigma_1)^2}{2}} \)

Beams, Shafts, and Plates

These are classic formulas you should know even if software does the work later.

Beam bending

\( \sigma = \frac{My}{I} \)
\( \frac{M}{I} = \frac{\sigma}{y} = \frac{E}{R} \)
Deflection relation
\( EI\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=M(x) \)
Torsion of circular shaft
\( \tau = \frac{Tr}{J} \)
\( \theta = \frac{TL}{JG} \)
Buckling load
\( P_{cr} = \frac{\pi^2 EI}{(KL)^2} \)

Vibrations
Mechanical Vibration Basics

Vibration analysis studies oscillatory motion and the system properties that control it.

Core quantities

\( x(t) = X\sin(\omega t + \phi) \)

General harmonic motion.

\( \omega = 2\pi f \)

Angular frequency and frequency relation.

\( T = \frac{1}{f} \)

Time period.

Vibrations
Single Degree of Freedom Systems

This is the base model for vibrations and one of the most important chapters in the site.

Undamped free vibration

\( m\ddot{x}+kx=0 \)
\( \omega_n = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \)
\( f_n = \frac{1}{2\pi}\sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \)
Damped free vibration
\( m\ddot{x}+c\dot{x}+kx=0 \)
\( c_c = 2\sqrt{km} \)
\( \zeta = \frac{c}{c_c} \)
\( \omega_d = \omega_n\sqrt{1-\zeta^2} \)
Forced vibration
\( m\ddot{x}+c\dot{x}+kx=F_0\sin(\omega t) \)
\( X = \frac{F_0/k}{\sqrt{(1-r^2)^2 + (2\zeta r)^2}} \)
\( r = \frac{\omega}{\omega_n} \)
Vibrations
Multiple Degree of Freedom Systems

Now the problem becomes matrix-based, which is where your linear algebra stops being decorative.

Matrix equation of motion

\( [M]\{\ddot{x}\} + [C]\{\dot{x}\} + [K]\{x\} = \{F(t)\} \)
Undamped eigenvalue problem
\( ([K]-\omega^2[M])\{\phi\}=0 \)

Solving this gives natural frequencies and mode shapes.

Vibrations
Modal Analysis

Modal analysis helps you understand how structures naturally prefer to vibrate.

Important relations

\( [\Phi]^T[M][\Phi] = [I] \)
\( [\Phi]^T[K][\Phi] = [\Omega^2] \)

Mode shape orthogonality lets you decouple the system into modal coordinates.

Frequency response function idea
\( H(\omega)=\frac{X(\omega)}{F(\omega)} \)
